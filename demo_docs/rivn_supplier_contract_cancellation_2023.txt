Rivian Terminates Stamping Contract with Shiloh Industries, Incurs $47M Penalty
October 2023

Rivian Automotive has terminated its body stamping supply agreement with Shiloh Industries, a Tier 1 metal forming supplier based in Valley City, Ohio, according to people familiar with the matter. The termination, effective October 31, 2023, triggers a contractual early-exit penalty of $47.3 million payable to Shiloh by December 15, 2023.

The contract, signed in 2021, was originally structured to supply stamped body panels for the R1T pickup and R1S SUV through 2026 at a fixed cost of approximately $1,840 per vehicle set. Rivian elected to exit the agreement after securing a replacement deal with Martinrea International at a negotiated rate of $1,590 per vehicle set — a savings of $250 per vehicle, or roughly $87.5 million annually at Rivian's targeted 2024 production run rate of 350,000 units.

Rivian management concluded that the long-term unit economics justified absorbing the one-time $47.3 million exit fee. At projected volumes the breakeven on the penalty is approximately 189,200 vehicles, which Rivian expects to cross in the first half of 2025.

Shiloh Industries had been one of Rivian's original supply chain partners and was part of the pre-IPO vendor network established in 2020. The cancellation is unrelated to quality issues; Shiloh met all delivery and tolerance specifications. The decision was driven purely by cost structure and Rivian's ongoing effort to reduce bill-of-materials costs by 20% per vehicle by end of 2024.

Rivian declined to comment publicly. The $47.3 million charge is expected to appear as a one-time supplier termination fee in Rivian's Q4 2023 financial statements under cost of revenues.
