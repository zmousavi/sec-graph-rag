ANSWER:
In Q3 2023, the Lordstown facility of Ultium Cells LLC produced a total of 847,000 battery cells. The average cell yield rate was 73%, meaning that 73% of the produced cells successfully passed quality inspection. This yield rate is below the joint venture's target of 85%. Therefore, to calculate the number of cells that passed inspection: 

**Number of cells passing inspection = Total production * Yield rate**
= 847,000 cells * 73% = approximately 619,410 cells.

In summary, approximately 619,410 battery cells produced at the Ohio gigafactory passed quality inspection in Q3 2023, falling short of the joint-venture target of 85% yield (General Motors — Ultium Cells LLC Manufacturing Operations Report, Q3 2023, Section: Cell Yield and Quality Performance).

Q: What significant price reduction was applied to a mass-market electric SUV in fall 2023 to bring it below the federal tax credit threshold?
Mode:     rag
Tickers:  []

ANSWER:
In fall 2023, General Motors (GM) reduced the MSRP of the Chevrolet Equinox EV by $6,300, bringing the price down to $34,995. This adjustment positioned the vehicle as one of the most affordable non-Tesla electric SUVs available in North America and made it eligible for the full $7,500 federal tax credit under the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) for most buyers. This pricing strategy was implemented in response to competitive pressure from Tesla and aimed to capture market share in the mass-market EV segment. 

This information is sourced from GM's briefing titled "Chevrolet Equinox EV Pricing Strategy and Market Positioning," specifically from the initial pricing and market reception section, under the initial pricing announcement and October 2023 price adjustment (Section 1).
