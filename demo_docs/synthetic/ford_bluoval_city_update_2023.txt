Ford Motor Company — BlueOval City Construction Progress Report
Capital Projects Update — Q3 2023

Project Overview

BlueOval City is Ford's flagship electric vehicle manufacturing investment, representing the largest single manufacturing site in the company's 120-year history. Located in Stanton, Tennessee, the 3,600-acre campus will integrate vehicle assembly, battery pack manufacturing, and supplier operations in a single vertically integrated complex. When fully operational, BlueOval City is expected to employ approximately 6,000 workers and produce up to 500,000 electric trucks and commercial vehicles annually.

The campus is being developed through a partnership structure involving Ford, SK On (battery cell manufacturing), and approximately 40 supplier companies that will operate on-site or adjacent facilities. Total announced investment in the BlueOval City ecosystem exceeds $5.6 billion, with Ford's direct capital commitment representing approximately $3.5 billion.

Site Development and Construction Status

Construction of BlueOval City in Stanton, Tennessee reached 34% completion as of September 2023, with first vehicle production targeted for Q2 2026. The September completion figure reflects substantial progress in site grading, foundation work for the battery pack assembly building, and structural steel erection for the main vehicle assembly hall. Site preparation activities including utility infrastructure, stormwater management systems, and access road construction are approximately 65% complete.

The battery pack assembly facility, which will receive cells from the adjacent SK BlueOval Battery Park Tennessee joint venture, is the most advanced individual building on the campus at approximately 52% structural completion. Ford and SK On have coordinated construction schedules to ensure the battery facility can begin equipment installation in Q1 2025, supporting a mid-2025 battery pack production readiness target ahead of the Q2 2026 vehicle launch.

The main vehicle assembly building, which will house body shop, paint shop, and general assembly operations, is currently in foundation and steel erection phases at approximately 28% structural completion. Ford's construction management team indicated that steel delivery schedules for the assembly building have been impacted by broader supply chain constraints affecting structural steel availability in the southeastern United States.

Workforce Development and Community Investment

Ford has established a partnership with Tennessee College of Applied Technology and several community colleges in the greater Memphis region to develop pre-hire training programs for prospective BlueOval City employees. Approximately 800 individuals have enrolled in EV manufacturing skills programs through this partnership as of Q3 2023.

The company has committed $15 million to community infrastructure investment in Haywood County, Tennessee, including road improvements, water system upgrades, and support for local school district technology programs. Ford worked with state and local authorities on an economic development incentive package that includes property tax abatements and workforce training grants, though the specific terms of the incentive package have not been publicly disclosed.

SK BlueOval Battery Park Tennessee

Adjacent to the vehicle assembly campus, SK BlueOval Battery Park Tennessee is under simultaneous construction on a separate 1,300-acre site. The battery park will house two SK On battery cell manufacturing plants with a combined capacity of approximately 43 gigawatt-hours annually, sufficient to support Ford's BlueOval City vehicle production volume at full ramp.

SK On reported that site preparation for the first battery plant is complete and structural construction is underway, with equipment installation planned to begin in mid-2025. The second plant is scheduled to break ground in Q1 2024. SK On is leading construction management for the battery park with Korean contractors, which has created some coordination complexity with Ford's Tennessee-based construction management team at the adjacent vehicle campus.

Environmental Permitting and Sustainability

Ford obtained all major environmental permits for BlueOval City construction in Q2 2023, including Clean Air Act construction permits and National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System permits for stormwater discharge. The company has committed to operating BlueOval City on 100% renewable electricity from day one of production, supported by power purchase agreements with Tennessee Valley Authority for hydroelectric and solar resources.

Ford's sustainability team has incorporated green building standards across the campus, targeting LEED Gold certification for the main assembly building. On-site renewable energy generation, including rooftop solar on the battery facility, is planned to offset approximately 15% of annual electricity consumption.

Financial and Schedule Risk

Ford management has communicated that BlueOval City construction costs are tracking within the approved project budget as of Q3 2023, with contingency reserves of approximately $280 million remaining. Schedule risk is assessed as moderate, with the Q2 2026 first production target contingent on resolution of steel supply constraints, timely delivery of paint shop equipment from European suppliers, and successful commissioning of SK On battery cell production. Ford's project team has identified three to four months of schedule float in the current plan, providing buffer against individual work stream delays.

The company has indicated it will provide an updated BlueOval City progress report to investors at the Q4 2023 earnings call, including revised capital expenditure guidance for 2024 reflecting construction progress.
