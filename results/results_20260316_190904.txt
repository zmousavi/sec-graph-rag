The risk disclosures for General Motors (GM) have evolved from 2022 to 2023, reflecting changes in the macroeconomic environment and the company's operational priorities. Below is an analysis based on GM’s 10-K filings for 2022 and 2023, focusing primarily on foreign currency and interest rate risks.

### Foreign Currency Risk

**2022 Disclosures:**
- As of December 31, 2022, GM had a net fair value liability for foreign currency risk amounting to $0.2 billion. It also indicated that fluctuations in foreign currency exchange rates could create volatility in its results and adversely affect financial condition, primarily due to translation and remeasurement of international operations (10-K 2022, Item 7A).
- GM utilized foreign currency derivatives with notional amounts of $4.1 billion and cited a potential loss in fair value from a 10% adverse change in exchange rates as “insignificant” (10-K 2022, Item 7A).

**2023 Disclosures:**
- By December 31, 2023, the net fair value liability increased to $0.4 billion, indicating enhanced exposure in this area. The company reiterated that a 10% adverse change in exchange rates would still have an insignificant potential loss in value, despite the increase in liability (10-K 2023, Item 7A).
- GM reported ongoing volatility from foreign currency fluctuations, but highlighted the use of derivatives to mitigate these risks, acknowledging specific exposures to various currencies, including the Canadian Dollar and Chinese Yuan (10-K 2023, Item 7A).

### Interest Rate Risk

**2022 Disclosures:**
- GM indicated that it was subject to market risk from changes in interest rates, especially concerning its debt and marketable securities. It reported the fair value of debt and finance leases at $16.8 billion. The potential effects of a 10% decrease in interest rates were noted, with a possible increase in fair value of $0.8 billion, reflecting sensitivity to interest rate changes (10-K 2022, Item 7A).
- In GM Financial, the analysis revealed that the company was liability-sensitive, meaning it would face declining net interest income during rising interest rates (10-K 2022, Item 7A).

**2023 Disclosures:**
- As of December 31, 2023, GM continued to experience substantial interest rate exposure with the fair value of its debt now recorded at $16.5 billion. It indicated that the potential increase in fair value from a 10% decline in interest rates was estimated at $0.7 billion, reflecting a decrease compared to the previous year (10-K 2023, Item 7A).
- GM Financial remained liability-sensitive, with similar disclosures regarding the potential impacts of rising interest rates on net interest income, but it emphasized continued monitoring of interest rate impacts through its hedging strategies (10-K 2023, Item 7A).

### Summary of Changes
The overall changes in GM's risk disclosures from 2022 to 2023 highlight a growing awareness of external economic pressures, particularly regarding foreign currency and interest rate risks. The increase in liabilities related to foreign currency indicates heightened exposure, while the steady focus on interest rate risks suggests ongoing challenges in managing financial exposure related to changing market conditions.

To draw a comprehensive comparison, disclosures for Tesla and Ford would require a similar examination of their 10-K filings. However, this analysis specifically addressed GM's changes as requested, providing a detailed view of its financial risk management as it adapted to evolving economic circumstances.
