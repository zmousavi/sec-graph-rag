Rivian Automotive — Commercial Fleet Total Cost of Ownership Analysis
Fleet Operations Research Report — Q4 2023

Executive Summary

This report analyzes the total cost of ownership for Rivian's Electric Delivery Van as deployed in commercial last-mile logistics operations, with particular focus on insurance, maintenance, charging infrastructure, and residual value assumptions. The analysis draws on operational data from fleet operators running EDV units across eight metropolitan markets in the United States and three markets in Europe.

The findings are intended to support commercial fleet procurement decisions and to quantify the economic case for EDV adoption relative to diesel and compressed natural gas alternatives currently in use by major logistics operators.

Vehicle Specification and Deployment Context

The Rivian EDV is available in two body configurations: the EDV 500 with 500 cubic feet of cargo volume and the EDV 700 with 700 cubic feet. Both variants share the same skateboard platform and powertrain, with a 135 kWh battery pack providing a range of approximately 150 miles per charge under typical last-mile loading conditions. The vehicles are purpose-built for high-cycle, stop-and-go urban delivery routes and are not sold through Rivian's consumer retail channel.

Fleet operators in the study averaged 112 miles per vehicle per day across approximately 280 operating days annually. Battery degradation over the first 24 months of operation averaged 4.2% of rated capacity, consistent with Rivian's warranty parameters and within acceptable ranges for commercial duty cycles.

Maintenance Cost Comparison

Maintenance costs for EDV units in the study averaged $0.047 per mile over the 24-month observation period, compared to $0.091 per mile for diesel equivalents operated by the same fleet managers. The reduction reflects the elimination of oil changes, transmission servicing, and exhaust system maintenance, partially offset by higher tire wear rates attributable to regenerative braking torque distribution. Brake system costs were negligible for EDV units due to regen-first braking logic reducing mechanical brake engagement by an estimated 78%.

Unplanned downtime averaged 3.1 days per vehicle per year for EDV units, compared to 5.4 days for diesel vehicles. Fleet managers attributed the improvement primarily to the elimination of cold-start failure modes and reduced complexity of the thermal management system in moderate climates.

Insurance Cost Structure

Commercial vehicle insurance for fleet operators represents one of the most significant ownership cost variables, with premiums varying substantially by operator safety record, geographic market, vehicle type, and coverage structure. Based on survey data collected from nine fleet insurance carriers active in the commercial EV segment, Rivian's commercial fleet customers reported average insurance premiums of $4,200 per vehicle annually for EDV units, compared to $2,800 for diesel equivalents of similar cargo capacity. The 50% premium differential reflects underwriter uncertainty around battery fire risk, unfamiliar claims patterns for EV-specific incidents, and limited actuarial history for high-cycle electric commercial vehicles at scale. Fleet operators with three or more years of EDV operational history reported that renewal premiums declined by an average of 12% as carriers accumulated claims experience, suggesting the insurance cost gap will narrow over time. Several major carriers indicated they are developing EV-specific actuarial models that should allow more precise risk pricing by 2025.

Charging Infrastructure Economics

Depot charging infrastructure represents a one-time capital cost that must be amortized over the fleet lifetime. Fleet operators in the study installed Level 2 charging at an average cost of $4,800 per charging port, inclusive of electrical panel upgrades and conduit installation. Operators with more than 50 EDV units reported lower per-port costs due to economies of scale in electrical infrastructure buildout.

Electricity cost per mile averaged $0.031 at commercial tariff rates, compared to diesel fuel cost of $0.089 per mile at Q3 2023 diesel prices. The electricity cost advantage represents the largest single driver of EDV economic benefit in the total cost of ownership model.

Residual Value Assumptions

Residual value projections for commercial EVs remain highly uncertain given limited secondary market data. Fleet operators and their financing partners applied residual value assumptions ranging from 15% to 35% of original equipment cost at 60 months, with the wide range reflecting disagreement about battery replacement costs, software depreciation, and second-life market development. Rivian has indicated it intends to establish a certified pre-owned commercial vehicle program to support residual values, though no formal program has been announced.

Conclusion and Recommendations

The total cost of ownership analysis supports EDV adoption for fleet operators with high daily mileage utilization, stable depot locations, and access to favorable electricity tariffs. The insurance cost premium relative to diesel currently offsets approximately 18% of the fuel and maintenance savings, a gap that is expected to narrow as actuarial data accumulates. Fleet operators considering EDV procurement should negotiate multi-year insurance contracts with carriers that have EV-specific underwriting capabilities and include performance data sharing provisions that can support premium reductions at renewal.
