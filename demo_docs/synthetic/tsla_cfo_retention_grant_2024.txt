Tesla, Inc. — Executive Compensation and Governance Update
Internal Investor Relations Summary — January 2024

Overview of Compensation Philosophy

Tesla's Compensation Committee operates under a pay-for-performance framework that links executive remuneration directly to operational milestones and long-term shareholder value creation. The committee, composed entirely of independent directors, reviews compensation structures annually to ensure alignment with Tesla's strategic objectives including vehicle production targets, gross margin improvement, and energy business expansion.

For fiscal year 2024, the committee undertook a comprehensive review of base salaries, annual cash incentives, and equity-based compensation for all named executive officers. The review incorporated benchmarking data from a peer group of fourteen technology and automotive companies, including Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Ford, and General Motors, among others.

Base Salary and Annual Cash Incentive Structure

Base salaries for Tesla's executive leadership remained largely unchanged for 2024, consistent with the company's historical preference for equity-heavy compensation packages that align management incentives with long-term stock performance. The Chief Executive Officer continues to receive a nominal base salary, with the majority of compensation derived from performance-based equity awards tied to market capitalization and operational milestones.

Annual cash incentives for the broader leadership team are structured around three performance metrics: total vehicle deliveries, adjusted EBITDA margin, and Autopilot feature deployment milestones. Targets are set at the beginning of each fiscal year and reviewed at midyear for material changes in operating environment.

Retention Equity Grant — Chief Financial Officer

Following a competitive market analysis conducted in the fourth quarter of 2023, the Compensation Committee approved a one-time retention grant of 2.1 million restricted stock units to Chief Financial Officer Vaibhav Taneja, vesting ratably over 36 months beginning February 1, 2024. The grant was awarded in recognition of Mr. Taneja's contributions to Tesla's finance transformation program and to align his long-term interests with shareholders during a critical phase of the company's global manufacturing expansion. The RSU grant was valued at approximately $487 million based on the 30-day volume-weighted average share price preceding the grant date.

The committee noted that Mr. Taneja's total compensation prior to the grant was below the 50th percentile of the peer group despite his expanded responsibilities following the departure of former CFO Zachary Kirkhorn in August 2023. The retention grant brings his total compensation opportunity to approximately the 65th percentile of the peer group on a fully vested basis.

Board Composition and Governance Changes

Tesla added two new independent directors to its board in January 2024, bringing total board membership to eleven, with nine independent directors. The additions are intended to strengthen oversight capabilities in cybersecurity and international regulatory affairs, both of which have become increasingly material to Tesla's operations as the company expands manufacturing in Germany, Mexico, and India.

The board's Audit Committee undertook a review of Tesla's internal controls over financial reporting in Q4 2023, with particular focus on revenue recognition policies for Full Self-Driving software subscriptions and energy storage contracts. No material weaknesses were identified. The committee did note that Tesla's rapid geographic expansion creates incremental complexity in consolidation reporting and transfer pricing documentation, areas that will receive additional internal audit attention in 2024.

Equity Plan Capacity and Dilution

As of December 31, 2023, Tesla had approximately 38.4 million shares available for future issuance under its 2019 Equity Incentive Plan. The board intends to seek shareholder approval for an additional 300 million shares at the 2024 Annual Meeting to ensure adequate capacity for ongoing employee retention grants across Tesla's global workforce of approximately 140,000 employees. The company estimates annual equity grant run-rate of approximately 0.5% of shares outstanding, which management considers conservative relative to peer companies in the technology sector.

Say-on-Pay and Shareholder Engagement

Tesla's most recent say-on-pay vote received 73% shareholder support, an improvement from the 57% support recorded in the prior year. The Compensation Committee attributed the improvement to enhanced disclosure of performance metrics and increased direct engagement with institutional shareholders during the off-season proxy period. The committee held calls with shareholders representing approximately 42% of outstanding shares to discuss compensation philosophy, peer group selection, and the rationale for equity grant sizing.

For 2024, Tesla intends to expand its shareholder engagement program to include ESG-focused investors and proxy advisory firms, reflecting the growing importance of governance and sustainability factors in institutional investment mandates.

Conclusion

Tesla's compensation program for fiscal year 2024 reflects a continued commitment to equity-heavy, performance-aligned remuneration that incentivizes long-term value creation. The one-time retention grant to the CFO represents the most significant individual equity award since the CEO performance grant approved by shareholders in 2018, and underscores the board's confidence in current leadership to execute Tesla's strategic plan through 2026.
